Case Study: Surviving the PDT Rule

The Pattern Day Trader (PDT) rule in the US requires traders with less than $25,000 in their account to make no more than 3 day trades in a rolling 5-day period. Many new traders get locked out after exceeding this limit.

  • Lesson: Always track your day trades and understand your broker's rules.
  • Strategy: Use swing trades or cash accounts to avoid PDT restrictions.
  • Risk: Violating the rule can result in account restrictions or forced liquidation.
